The era of Mac gaming being a punchline is over. Apple Silicon chips deliver enough raw GPU power to run demanding native titles, and developers are finally shipping Mac builds day-one. Here are the best native Mac games worth playing in 2026.

## Why native matters

Native games compile directly for Apple Silicon and use Metal, Apple's graphics API. The result is lower latency, better battery life on laptops, zero compatibility overhead, and access to macOS game features like Game Mode and Optimised for Apple Silicon builds.

Steam now reports that over 30% of its Mac catalog runs natively on Apple Silicon, up from under 10% in 2022.

## AAA titles with native Mac support

**Baldur's Gate 3** — Larian Studios shipped a native Apple Silicon build and it is one of the best optimised ports available. Runs beautifully on M2 and above, even on a 14-inch MacBook Pro.

**Hades** — Supergiant Games has always been Mac-first. Hades and Hades II run flawlessly on Apple Silicon and are among the most polished experiences on the platform.

**Disco Elysium** — Another Supergiant title that runs natively. A masterclass in writing and runs on literally any Apple Silicon Mac.

**Return of the Obra Dinn** — Lucas Pope's detective masterpiece runs on a MacBook Air and still looks gorgeous.

**Stray** — The cat simulator got a native Mac port and it showcases Apple Silicon's GPU capabilities with its detailed environments.

## Indie gems running native

The indie scene is where Mac gaming truly shines. Most indie developers ship Universal binaries that run on both Intel and Apple Silicon:

- **Hollow Knight** — One of the best metroidvanias ever made, runs perfectly
- **Stardew Valley** — Cozy farming sim, native on Mac
- **Celeste** — Precision platformer, buttery smooth on M-series chips
- **Dead Cells** — Roguelite action that runs great even on base M2
- **Terraria** — Sandbox adventure, native and affordable
- **Vampire Survivors** — Addictive reverse-roguelite, runs on anything
- **Slay the Spire** — The deckbuilding roguelite that started it all
- **Among Us** — Social deduction, native multiplayer on Mac
- **Untitled Goose Game** — Chaotic fun, native on Apple Silicon

## Games to avoid on Mac right now

Not everything with a Mac store page runs well. Be cautious of:
- Intel-only builds that run through Rosetta 2 (they work but are not optimised)
- Titles labelled "Optimised for Apple Silicon" that are actually just Rosetta 2 wrappers
- Games from publishers that have abandoned Mac support (looking at you, EA)

## Where to buy

**Steam** has the largest native Mac library. Filter by "SteamOS + Linux" and check the Mac requirements section for Apple Silicon support.

**Apple Arcade** offers 200+ ad-free games for $10.99/month AUD. Great for casual gaming and family-friendly titles.

**Epic Games Store** has a growing Mac catalog, though smaller than Steam.

**GOG** offers DRM-free native Mac builds for many classic and indie titles.

## Hardware recommendations for native gaming

- **MacBook Air M2/M3** — Handles indie and most AAA titles at 1080p. Fanless design means no thermal throttling noise.
- **MacBook Pro 14-inch M3 Pro** — The sweet spot for serious Mac gaming. Active cooling, excellent GPU, great screen.
- **Mac Studio M3 Max** — Overkill for most games, but if you want to play at the highest settings on a 4K display, this is your machine.
- **Mac Mini M2/M3** — Budget gaming. Pair it with a 4K display and you have a console-like setup for under $2,000 AUD.

## The outlook for 2026

Apple's Game Porting Toolkit 2 makes it easier for developers to bring DirectX 12 games to macOS using Metal. Expect more AAA titles with native Mac support throughout 2026, especially from studios that already support Linux.

The native Mac gaming ecosystem is real, growing, and worth investing in if you own an Apple Silicon Mac.
