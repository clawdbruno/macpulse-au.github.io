Mac Studio M3 Ultra with 512GB RAM — Is the $19,490 Price Tag Justified?

30 June 2026· map[name:Mac Pulse Team]

Apple’s Mac Studio with the M3 Ultra chip and 512GB of unified memory sits at the top of the Mac lineup, priced at $19,490 AUD through the Apple Store. That is a serious ask, even for a professional workstation.

What you get

The M3 Ultra packs up to 32 CPU cores and 80 GPU cores, with a memory bandwidth of 800GB/s. The 512GB unified memory configuration means you can keep massive datasets, 8K video timelines, and complex 3D scenes in memory simultaneously without swapping to disk.

For comparison, a similarly specced Intel-based Mac Pro would have cost over $25,000 and drawn enough power to heat a small room.

Who actually needs 512GB of RAM

Let us be honest: most people do not. Even most professionals do not.

This configuration makes sense if you:

  • Edit 8K RAW video across multiple timelines in Final Cut Pro or DaVinci Resolve
  • Run local machine learning models or AI workloads that require massive context windows
  • Work with large 3D scenes in Blender, Cinema 4D, or Maya with complex textures
  • Compile massive Xcode projects with multiple simulator instances
  • Run virtualisation workloads with multiple VMs simultaneously

If you are a photographer, writer, web developer, or even a 4K video editor, the M3 Max with 128GB RAM will serve you just as well at a fraction of the cost.

The Australian deal situation

Mainstream Australian retailers like JB Hi-Fi and Harvey Norman rarely stock this configuration. Your best bets are:

  • Apple Certified Refurbished — occasionally lists high-spec Mac Studios at 15% off, with the full one-year warranty
  • eBay Australia — private sellers sometimes list open-box or barely-used units
  • MacRefurb and MacConnection — worth checking weekly for stock drops

We will be monitoring prices hourly and alerting readers when genuine deals appear.

The verdict

At full RRP, the Mac Studio M3 Ultra 512GB is a luxury purchase even for professionals. The performance per dollar drops sharply past the M3 Max tier. However, if you catch one on Apple’s refurbished store at around $16,500, it becomes one of the best deals in the pro computing space.

Patience pays here. These machines do not go out of spec quickly, and refurbished units appear regularly.
